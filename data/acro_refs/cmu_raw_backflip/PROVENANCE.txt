CANDIDATE 3 (fallback, needs our own retarget): CMU Graphics Lab raw backflip mocap
===================================================================================

SOURCE
  CMU Graphics Lab Motion Capture Database, http://mocap.cs.cmu.edu
  Direct URLs:
    http://mocap.cs.cmu.edu/subjects/87/87.asf      (skeleton, subject 87)
    http://mocap.cs.cmu.edu/subjects/87/87_03.amc   "Backflip"
    http://mocap.cs.cmu.edu/subjects/88/88.asf      (skeleton, subject 88)
    http://mocap.cs.cmu.edu/subjects/88/88_01.amc   "backflip"
    http://mocap.cs.cmu.edu/subjects/88/88_05.amc   "cartwheel into backflip"
  Trial descriptions from the CMU index (mirror:
    https://github.com/una-dinosauria/cmu-mocap/blob/master/cmu-mocap-index-text.txt).
  Subjects 85-90 are the acrobatics/flips subjects (85 breakdance/flips, 87/88/89/90
  acrobatics). ASF header names the performer session "Acrobat_Osman".
  LICENSE: free for all uses, including commercial ("This data is free for use in research
  projects. You may include this data in commercially-sold products, ... please send the
  citation: mocap.cs.cmu.edu, funded by NSF EIA-0196217").

FORMAT
  ASF/AMC (Acclaim), CMU 31-bone human skeleton, Euler angles in degrees, root pos+rot.
  Nominal capture rate 120 fps (some subject-88 trials are 60 fps -- the fleaven AMASS-G1
  dataset labels 88_10/88_11 as 60 fps; verify per-trial when converting).
  Frame counts (lines matching /^[0-9]+$/): 87_03 = 247, 88_01 = 202, 88_05 = 149
  (~2.1 s / 1.7 s / 1.2 s at 120 fps, or 2x that if 60 fps).

WHY CHOSEN
  Clean studio optical mocap of true standing backflips (vs. the noisier video-mocap
  KungfuAthleteBot clips) with an unrestricted license. NOT yet retargeted to G1 -- this is
  the "retarget ourselves" fallback if the KungfuAthleteBot flips prove too noisy.

CONVERTIBILITY TO OUR 36-col 30fps CSV: MODERATE EFFORT (needs retargeting)
  Options:
  a) AMC -> BVH (cgspeed's MotionBuilder-friendly BVH conversion of these trials exists;
     mirrors: outworldz.com/Secondlife/Posts/CMU/) -> GMR (YanjieZe/GMR, supports BVH input)
     -> G1 pkl -> csv (GMR ships retarget/scripts/pkl_2_csv.py producing the BeyondMimic
     36-col CSV = our format).
  b) AMASS SMPL-X version of CMU trials (registration-walled at amass.is.tue.mpg.de) -> GMR
     smplx pathway. NOTE: the freely-mirrored AMASS-retargeted-G1 datasets
     (fleaven/Retargeted_AMASS_for_robotics, ember-lab-berkeley/AMASS_Retargeted_for_G1)
     DROPPED all flip trials (87_03, 88_01, 88_05, 90_19 etc. absent) -- only non-flip
     trials of subjects 85-90 survived their filtering. So no shortcut there.
  c) Our existing GVHMR pipeline (cloud/10_gvhmr.sh) does not apply (no video here); but a
     video-of-mocap render could be fed through it in a pinch.
