WHY CHOSEN
  Only freely downloadable, already-G1-retargeted, 29-DoF STANDING BACKFLIP reference found
  anywhere (ASAP, PBHC examples, LAFAN1-retarget, AMASS-G1 retargets all contain no flips).
  Source video title: 原地后空翻+腾空飞脚180°+蝎式 = "standing backflip + 180 deg flying
  kick + scorpion pose". 278 = Scene-002, 280 = Scene-005 of that video ("Action File
  Title.csv" in the dataset root maps ids -> video titles). The repo's own demo GIF
  (docs/example_g1_after_278.gif) is clip 278. Dataset Skills category: Backflip(3),
  Side Flip(35), Air Spin(18), Lotus Swing(13); ids 278 & 280 are the backflip clips
  that survived their quality filtering (276/277/279/281 were dropped upstream).

FORMAT (verified locally with numpy)
  *_org.npz: { fps: int64 scalar = 30, qpos: (T,36) float64 }  MuJoCo qpos for G1 29-DoF:
    qpos[0:3] = root xyz (m), qpos[3:7] = root quaternion in WXYZ (w first; frame0 ~ [0.88,...]),
    qpos[7:36] = 29 joint angles in EXACTLY our target order (verified against joint_names in
    *_smooth_mj.npz: left_hip_pitch...left_ankle_roll, right leg, waist y/r/p, left arm 7,
    right arm 7 -- identical to LAFAN1_Retargeting_Dataset / BeyondMimic CSV order).
  *_smooth_mj.npz: fps=50, joint_pos (T-1,36), joint_vel (T-1,35), body_pos_w/body_quat_w/
    body_lin_vel_w/body_ang_vel_w (T-1,53,3|4), joint_names (29), body_names (53).

DURATION / FLIP EVIDENCE (computed from *_org.npz)
  278: 104 frames @30fps = 3.47 s. Root fully inverted (body z-axis dot world z < -0.5)
       frames 43-47 (t = 1.43-1.57 s); airborne z>0.85 m frames 34-53; peak root z 1.15 m;
       sagittal tilt sweep 265 deg (flip + scorpion ending, ends low z=0.36).
  280: 96 frames @30fps = 3.20 s. Inverted frames 31-34 (t = 1.03-1.13 s); cumulative
       backward pitch sweep -448 deg (full 360 flip + landing recovery); peak root z 0.97 m.
       280 is the cleanest pure standing-backflip segment of the two.

CONVERTIBILITY TO OUR 36-col 30fps CSV: TRIVIAL
  org npz is already 30 fps, 36-wide qpos with our joint order. Only change: our CSV stores
  the root quaternion as x,y,z,w in cols 3-6 (loader: qpos[3]=m[:,6], qpos[4:7]=m[:,3:6]),
  while the npz qpos[3:7] is w,x,y,z. So per frame write:
    csv_row = [x, y, z, qx, qy, qz, qw, j1..j29] = qpos[[0,1,2,4,5,6,3,7..35]]

CAVEATS
  - Video mocap (GVHMR): jump subset flagged by authors as having "minor imperfections";
    inspect in MuJoCo before training. Repo provides retarget/scripts/
    adjust_robot_height_by_gravity.py for ground-height fixing.
  - Kinematic reference only; human backflip timing may need time-scaling for G1 dynamics.
  - "smoothed" file has fps=50 with unchanged frame count vs org (30fps) -> effectively a
    1.67x speed-up if played at face value; prefer org (30 fps) and re-verify.
